  • Social Media has changed a lot before and after the outbreak of the Coronavirus.

Apps
Tiktok

TikTok originated as an app called Musical.ly, where users would create videos lip-syncing to songs. After being acquired and rebranded, TikTok has emerged as a highly popular platform for both users and content creators alike.

Twitch

Twitch is a live streaming platform primarily focused on video gaming, allowing users to broadcast their gameplay, watch others play, and interact with streamers through live chat. Over the years, Twitch has expanded beyond gaming to include a wide range of content, including music, talk shows, and creative arts. With millions of active users and a vibrant community, Twitch has become one of the leading platforms for live streaming and content creation.

YouTube

YouTube is a video-sharing platform where users can upload, share, and view videos. It has become one of the largest and most popular video platforms on the internet, offering a wide range of content including music videos, vlogs, tutorials, and more. With billions of users and hours of video content uploaded every minute, YouTube has a significant impact on internet culture and entertainment.
